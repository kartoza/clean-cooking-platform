The Science Behind Clean Cooking Explorer

Multi-Criteria Analysis Using the Clean Cooking Explorer Tool

CCE includes a multi-criteria analysis tool that uses location-specific resource availability and infrastructure data to represent clean cooking energy supply. It also incorporates demographic data and data on social and productive uses to visualize demand for clean cooking solutions. Together, these supply and demand indicators enable more comprehensive energy planning. Spatial analysis tools, including multi-criteria analysis, overlays, filters, and buffer zones, help users identify and prioritize areas to enable the adoption of clean cooking solutions.

The tool incorporates remote sensing data as well as data from global, national, sub-national and census databases that are either publicly available or provided by international partners and local stakeholders. It can host data available in various resolutions, scales and formats allowing for better or new datasets to be incorporated once available. The selection of data is based on a literature review, international and local stakeholder engagement, and surveys conducted on the importance of certain datasets, relevant actors, tools and analyses in geospatial clean cooking planning. The list of essential data is reviewed by local stakeholders to ensure the platform is relevant and applicable in the local context.
