Why Clean Cooking Explorer?

Access to clean cooking technology (such as gas or electric stoves) can greatly improve health outcomes. Approximately 3 billion people depend on biomass fuels such as wood, coal, and dung for cooking to cook with (source: WHO article on household air pollution). The indoor air pollution generated whilst cooking indoors has serious, negative health impacts, with women and children in particular being affected.

The CCE is an online, open-source, and interactive spatial platform for promoting clean cooking throughout a country. The spatial platform integrates and supports analysis of geospatial datasets related to both the potential demand and supply of clean cooking services, allow data-driven planning, coordination, and decision-making to support the uptake and adoption of clean cooking throughout the country. This platform will empower users to visualize, interpret, and analyze geospatial datasets to accelerate access to clean cooking.


How

To understand where access to clean cooking technology is needed we can combine spatial data, economic factors and sophisticated analysis tools. These tools provided on this site will help planners to answer these key questions:

  • Where is the demand for Clean Cooking Technology?

  • What Clean Cooking Technology is cost-optimal in each location?

Understand the reports

The CCE synthesizes location-specific data, enables online geospatial analysis for assessing demand and supply in the clean cooking sector, and allows to share relevant information for a holistic approach in the clean cooking sector.

The use of the CCE platform will enable decision making, planning, assessing and visualizing, and data driven coordination.


The Clean Cooking Explorer is Open Source and can be freely downloaded and deployed on your own server. Visit our GitHub page for the source.
